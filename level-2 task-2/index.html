Biography

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was a visionary American entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc. Born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, Jobs revolutionized the personal computer industry. Co-founding Apple in 1976, he left briefly but returned in 1996, steering the company to unprecedented success with iconic products like the iPhone and iPad. Known for his relentless pursuit of innovation and design perfection, Jobs transformed Apple into a global tech giant. His influential leadership left an indelible mark on the technology landscape. Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, leaving a legacy of innovation and creativity that continues to shape the industry.
